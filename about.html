I am a sophomore at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology double majoring in computer science and applied mathematics.

I am passionate about applying computer science to solve global issues, with a recent focus on machine learning research. I am currently investigating the stability of of deep networks at MIT's Center for Brains, Minds, and Machines. I have previously worked on unsupervised 3D mesh segmentation at IBM Research, high-throughput omics for cardiovascular disease progression at Fondazione Bruno Kessler in Trento, Italy, and the detection of tumors in mammograms at the George Washington University in Washington, D.C. In the future, I hope to expand my scope of work to include robotics and energy.

Outside of academics, I am interested in investigating the intersection of art, media, and technology through mediums such as graphic design, UI/UX, and fashion. At MIT, I am currently the Co-President of Women Business Leaders (WBL), Co-Director of AI@MIT's Generator Competition, Vice Chair of the UA Diversity Council, and a Sourcing Principal at Harvard Undergraduate Capital Partners. I'm also involved with the Asian American Initiative and the Mirchi and MissBehavior dance teams. In my (quite limited) free time, you can catch me reading, playing classical piano, making zines, or learning a new hobby.

Feel free to hit me up if you're interested in working together, have questions, or just want to grab a coffee.
