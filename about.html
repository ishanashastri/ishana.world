about

I am a rising sophomore at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, with an intended double major in computer science and mathematics.

A 2019 graduate from Poolesville High School's Science, Math, and Computer Science magnet program, I am interested in applying computer science to solve global issues, with a recent focus on machine learning research. I am currently working on theoretical machine learning at MIT's Center for Brains, Minds, and Machines. I have previously worked on unsupervised 3D mesh segmentation at IBM Research, high-throughput omics for cardiovascular disease progression at Fondazione Bruno Kessler in Trento, Italy, and the detection of tumors in mammograms at the George Washington University in Washington, D.C. In the future, I hope to expand my scope of work to include robotics and energy.

Outside of academics, I am interested in investigating the intersection of art, media, and technology through mediums such as graphic design, UI/UX, and fashion.
